Cargo Ship Vanishes Off Coast; No Distress Signal Sent

By NEWS 6 Newsroom Staff  |  Filed 11:47 PM, June 27, 2003

A cargo vessel sank off the coast late Friday night in what officials are calling an unusual and still-unexplained incident. Local fishermen observing the ship through binoculars reported that it went under in less than a minute, with no visible signs of listing or structural failure prior to submersion.

No distress call was received from the vessel. Coast Guard radio contact could not be established either before or after the sinking. Rescue crews were dispatched to the area but have not yet located wreckage or survivors.

Approximately five hours after the ship went down, the surrounding region experienced seismic activity strong enough to damage several buildings in nearby coastal towns. Seismologists have not commented on any possible connection between the two events. NEWS 6 aired a special emergency broadcast covering both developments as they happened.

Authorities Investigate Late-Night Gathering at Old Mill Road

Police responded to reports of a large group gathered on private property near the old grain mill off Route 9 shortly before midnight. Witnesses described chanting and a bonfire visible from the road. No arrests were made, and the group had dispersed by the time officers arrived. This is the third such report in the area this month. Neighbors have asked the county to increase patrols in the area.
