Markets Slide as Tech Sector Jitters Continue
By NEWS 6 Newsroom Staff | Filed 4:10 PM, June 27, 2003
Stocks closed lower for the third straight session Friday, with the Nasdaq shedding 1.8% amid renewed concern over technology-sector earnings.

Analysts remain divided on whether the pullback signals a broader correction or a routine adjustment following recent gains. Trading volume was described as "moderate" by floor sources.

The Dow and S&P 500 also finished in negative territory, though losses there were more modest. Bond yields ticked slightly higher on the session.

Market watchers say next week's earnings reports from several large technology firms will likely set the tone heading into the summer.
